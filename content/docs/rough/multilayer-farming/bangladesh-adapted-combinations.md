# 🇧🇩 Multi-Layer Farming — Bangladesh Adapted Combinations

> Adapted from Akash Chaurasia's 4-layer technique for Bangladesh's tropical climate, soil types, and locally available crops.

---

## Why Bangladesh is Perfect for Multilayer Farming

| Advantage | Details |
|---|---|
| **Climate** | Tropical — warm year-round → longer growing seasons, faster crop cycles |
| **Rainfall** | Abundant monsoon (1500–3000mm/year) → less irrigation dependency |
| **Bamboo** | Grows everywhere, cheapest structural material for the support frame |
| **Biodiversity** | Huge variety of local vegetables, fruits, and root crops available |
| **Small landholdings** | Average farm is 0.6 hectares — multilayer maximizes this limited space |
| **Labor** | Family labor available — multilayer is labor-intensive but manageable for family farms |

## Bangladesh-Adapted Layer Model

```
Layer 5 (Tall Canopy):   🌴 Coconut / Areca nut / Mango [BD addition]
                          ↕ 15-30 ft
Layer 4 (Medium Tree):   🌳 Papaya / Guava / Drumstick (সজনে)
                          ↕ 8-12 ft
Layer 3 (Vine/Creeper):  🌿 Bitter gourd / Bottle gourd / Country bean (শিম)
                          ↕ 6-7 ft (bamboo structure)
Layer 2 (Ground):         🥬 Red amaranth / Spinach / Coriander / Malabar spinach
                          ↕ 1-2 ft
Layer 1 (Underground):    🫚 Ginger / Turmeric / Taro (কচু)
                          ↕ below surface
```

**Bangladesh adds a 5th layer** — tall palms/trees that don't exist in Akash's drier MP climate but thrive in BD's tropics.

## Recommended Combinations (by Bangladesh Season)

### Combination 1: Year-Round Homestead Model

Best for: Homestead garden (বসতবাড়ির বাগান), 5–15 decimal

| Layer | Crop | Local Name | Harvest Cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Underground | Turmeric | হলুদ | 8–9 months |
| Ground | Red amaranth + Coriander | লাল শাক + ধনেপাতা | 30–40 days (repeat) |
| Vine | Country bean | শিম | 90–120 days |
| Medium tree | Papaya | পেঁপে | 10–12 months |
| Tall canopy | Coconut | নারিকেল | Perennial (3+ years to fruit) |

**ZBNF integration:** Jeevamrutha at root zone every 15 days. Beejamrutha for all seeds. Mulch with coconut husks and banana leaves.

### Combination 2: Rabi Season (Winter) — Nov to Feb

Best for: Maximum winter vegetable production

| Layer | Crop | Local Name | Harvest Cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Underground | Radish (deep root) | মুলা | 40–50 days |
| Ground | Spinach + Fenugreek | পালং + মেথি | 30–45 days |
| Vine | Bottle gourd | লাউ | 70–90 days |
| Medium tree | Drumstick | সজনে | Perennial (leaves + pods) |

**Why this works in winter:** Cool temperatures favor leafy greens and radish. Bottle gourd thrives in moderate warmth. Drumstick leaves are a superfood — harvest leaves continuously.

### Combination 3: Kharif Season (Monsoon) — Jun to Oct

Best for: Monsoon-tolerant crops on raised beds

| Layer | Crop | Local Name | Harvest Cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Underground | Taro/Kachu | কচু | 5–6 months |
| Ground | Kangkong (water spinach) | কলমি শাক | 30 days (repeat) |
| Vine | Pointed gourd | পটল | 90–120 days |
| Medium tree | Banana | কলা | 10–14 months |

**Monsoon tip:** Use **raised beds** (at least 6 inches) to prevent waterlogging. Kangkong loves moisture — perfect monsoon ground cover.

### Combination 4: Pre-Monsoon/Hot Season — Mar to May

Best for: Heat-tolerant crops

| Layer | Crop | Local Name | Harvest Cycle |
|---|---|---|---|
| Underground | Ginger | আদা | 8–9 months (plant now, harvest winter) |
| Ground | Malabar spinach | পুঁই শাক | 45–60 days (repeat) |
| Vine | Ridge gourd + Snake gourd | ঝিঙা + চিচিঙ্গা | 60–80 days |
| Medium tree | Papaya | পেঁপে | 10–12 months |

**Heat tip:** Thick mulch is critical. The vine canopy on the bamboo structure provides natural shade for ground crops.

### Combination 5: Fish + Multilayer (Gher System Integration)

Best for: Southern Bangladesh (Khulna, Bagerhat), pond embankments

| Layer | Crop/Animal | Location |
|---|---|---|
| Water | Fish (tilapia, rui, katla) | Pond |
| Embankment ground | Spinach + Radish + Onion | Pond bank |
| Embankment vine | Bottle gourd + Country bean | Trellis on bank |
| Embankment tree | Papaya + Guava | Edges of bank |

**Cycle:** Fish waste enriches water → irrigate crops with pond water → crop residue feeds fish → circular system.

## Crop Compatibility Rules (Bangladesh Climate)

### ✅ Good Companions

| Crop A | Crop B | Why They Work Together |
|---|---|---|
| Turmeric (underground) | Spinach (ground) | Different root depths, no competition |
| Country bean (vine) | Red amaranth (ground) | Bean fixes nitrogen, amaranth benefits |
| Papaya (tree) | Ginger (underground) | Papaya shade protects ginger from direct sun |
| Drumstick (tree) | Fenugreek (ground) | Light shade from drumstick benefits fenugreek |
| Banana (tree) | Taro (underground) | Banana shade + moisture suits taro perfectly |
| Bottle gourd (vine) | Coriander (ground) | Gourd canopy filters harsh light for coriander |

### ❌ Bad Companions (Avoid)

| Crop A | Crop B | Why They Conflict |
|---|---|---|
| Two vine crops on same structure | — | Compete for structure space and light |
| Tomato | Heavy shade tree | Tomatoes need full sun |
| Potato | Ginger (same layer) | Both are underground, compete for same soil space |
| Brinjal | Heavy shade | Brinjal needs full sun for flowering |

## Integration with ZBNF

| ZBNF Practice | How It Fits Multilayer |
|---|---|
| Jeevamrutha | Apply at root zone of each layer separately. Trees get more, greens get less. |
| Beejamrutha | Treat ALL seeds — ground layer, vine layer, and tree saplings |
| Mulching | Mulch the entire ground between plants. Vine canopy acts as living mulch above. |
| Neemastra | Spray on vine and ground layers. Trees usually need less (harder for pests to reach) |
| Whapasa | Drip irrigation maintains moisture balance across all layers without waterlogging |
