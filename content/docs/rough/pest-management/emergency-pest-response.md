# 🪲 ZBNF Pest Management — Full Lifecycle Guide

> **No market pesticides. No chemicals. Only ZBNF on-farm formulations and ecological methods.**

---

## The ZBNF Pest Philosophy

In ZBNF, pests are not "enemies to destroy" — they are signals of an imbalanced ecosystem. The goal is NOT to kill every insect but to:

1. **Prevent** attacks through healthy soil and plant immunity
2. **Detect** early through regular observation
3. **Respond** with escalating ZBNF formulations
4. **Recover** by restoring ecosystem balance

---

## Stage 1: Prevention (Always Active)

Prevention is 80% of pest management. A healthy ZBNF farm naturally resists pests.

### 1A. Build Plant Immunity Through Soil Health

| Practice | How It Prevents Pests |
|---|---|
| **Jeevamrutha every 15 days** | Strong microbial activity → plants absorb nutrients better → stronger cell walls → harder for pests to penetrate |
| **Beejamrutha seed treatment** | Protective microbial coating → seedlings resist soil-borne pathogens from day 1 |
| **Mulching** | Habitat for beneficial predators (spiders, beetles). Keeps soil cool and microbially active. |
| **Whapasa (moisture balance)** | Not too wet, not too dry → prevents root rot, fungal attacks |

### 1B. Companion Planting (Pest Repellent Plants)

Plant these alongside your main crops:

| Companion Plant | What It Repels | Plant Near |
|---|---|---|
| **Marigold (গাঁদা ফুল)** | Nematodes, whiteflies, aphids | Tomato, brinjal, beans |
| **Basil (তুলসী)** | Flies, mosquitoes, thrips | Tomato, pepper |
| **Lemongrass (লেমনগ্রাস)** | Mosquitoes, many flying insects | Border crop around any plot |
| **Coriander (ধনেপাতা)** | Aphids, spider mites | Leafy vegetables |
| **Garlic (রসুন)** | Aphids, fungal diseases | Roses, tomatoes, cabbage |
| **Neem tree (নিম)** | Nearly all pests | Plant at plot corners |

### 1C. Trap Crops (Sacrificial Lure)

Trap crops attract pests away from your main crop:

| Trap Crop | Pest It Attracts | Use With |
|---|---|---|
| **Mustard (সরিষা)** | Aphids, diamond-back moth | Cabbage, cauliflower |
| **Castor (রেড়ি)** | Leaf-eating caterpillars | Cotton, vegetables |
| **Sunflower (সূর্যমুখী)** | Stink bugs, bollworms | Most crops |
| **Nasturtium** | Aphids, whiteflies | Beans, tomatoes |

Plant trap crops in a row along the border or between plots. When pests congregate on them, you can hand-pick or spray with Neemastra.

### 1D. Encourage Natural Predators

These insects are your FREE pest control army:

| Predator | What It Eats | How to Attract |
|---|---|---|
| **Ladybugs** | Aphids (1 ladybug = 50 aphids/day) | Don't spray chemicals. Plant dill, fennel, yarrow. |
| **Spiders** | Flying insects, small caterpillars | Maintain mulch layer — spiders live in mulch. |
| **Dragonflies** | Mosquitoes, small flies | Keep a small water body nearby. |
| **Parasitic wasps** | Caterpillars (lay eggs inside them) | Plant small-flowered plants (coriander, dill, fennel). |
| **Frogs/toads** | Slugs, beetles, many insects | Maintain moist areas near plot, don't use chemicals. |
| **Birds** | Caterpillars, grasshoppers | Plant trees nearby, put up bird perches. |

**Rule:** If you see ladybugs, spiders, and wasps in your field — your ecosystem is healthy. Do NOT spray anything that kills them.

## Stage 2: Early Detection (Weekly)

**Check your crops every week.** The earlier you spot a problem, the easier it is to handle.

### What to Look For

| Sign | Likely Pest | Where to Check |
|---|---|---|
| Curled/distorted leaves | Aphids, thrips | Underside of young leaves |
| White powdery coating | Whiteflies | Lower leaf surface |
| Holes in leaves | Caterpillars, beetles | Both sides of leaf, also check soil near stem |
| Sticky residue on leaves | Aphids, mealybugs | Leaf surface, ants nearby = confirmation |
| Wilting despite adequate water | Root borers, bacterial wilt | Base of stem, dig gently around roots |
| Yellowing + tiny dots | Spider mites | Underside of leaves with magnifying glass |
| Bored holes in stems/fruits | Stem/fruit borer | Stem joints, fruit surface |
| White egg clusters | Upcoming caterpillar attack | Underside of leaves |

### Severity Scoring

| Level | Description | Urgency |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 None/Minimal | 0–5% plants affected, natural predators visible | No action. Ecosystem is managing it. |
| 🟡 Moderate | 10–20% plants affected | Targeted Neemastra spray on affected areas |
| 🔴 Heavy | 20–40% plants affected | Full plot Neemastra + prepare Agniastra |
| 🔴🔴 Severe/Crisis | 40%+ plants affected, spreading fast | Agniastra + Brahmastra. See Emergency Response below. |

## Stage 3: Emergency Response (When Pests Attack After ZBNF)

**You've done everything right — Jeevamrutha, mulching, companion planting — but pests still attacked. Here's the escalation protocol using ONLY ZBNF on-farm formulations.**

### Level 1: Neemastra (First Response)

**For:** Sucking pests (aphids, jassids, whiteflies, mealybugs), small caterpillars

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Water | 200L |
| Cow urine | 10L |
| Cow dung | 2 kg |
| Neem leaf paste | 10 kg |

**Prep:** Mix → ferment 48 hrs (stir 2x daily) → filter → spray directly (no dilution)
**Frequency:** Every 3–5 days until pest population drops

### Level 2: Agniastra (Escalation)

**For:** Tougher pests — stem borers, fruit borers, leaf rollers, pod borers

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Cow urine | 20L |
| Neem leaf paste | 2 kg |
| Tobacco powder | 500g |
| Green chilli paste | 500g |
| Garlic paste | 250g |

**Prep:** Mix all in cow urine → **boil on slow flame until foam** → cool 48 hrs in shade → filter
**Application:** Dilute 6–8L concentrate in 200L water → spray on crops
**Frequency:** Every 5–7 days until controlled

### Level 3: Brahmastra (Heavy Artillery)

**For:** Severe infestations, hidden caterpillars in pods/fruits, resistant pests

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Cow urine | 20L |
| Paste of 5 different bitter/aromatic leaves (2 kg each): neem, karanj, custard apple, datura, castor, mango, or lantana | 10 kg total |

**Prep:** Make leaf paste → add to cow urine → boil on slow flame → cool 48 hrs → filter
**Application:** Dilute 6L in 200L water → spray on crops
**Frequency:** Every 7 days, maximum 3 applications

### Level 4: Dashparni Ark (Last Resort — 10-Plant Extract)

**For:** The most severe, uncontrollable infestations

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|---|---|
| Cow urine | 20L |
| Neem leaves | 3 kg |
| Karanj (pongamia) leaves | 2 kg |
| Custard apple leaves | 2 kg |
| Castor leaves | 2 kg |
| Madar/Aak (Calotropis) leaves | 2 kg |
| Datura leaves | 2 kg |
| Tobacco leaves | 1 kg |
| Green chilli paste | 500g |
| Garlic paste | 500g |

**Prep:** Crush all leaves → soak in cow urine for 30 days (yes, one month) → stir every 3 days → filter
**Application:** Dilute 3–5L in 200L water → spray
**Note:** This is extremely potent. Prepare in advance and keep ready.

## Stage 4: Recovery (After the Attack)

After controlling the pest, rebuild your farm's defense:

- [ ] Apply **extra Jeevamrutha** (every 7 days for 1 month) to boost soil microbial recovery
- [ ] Add **fresh mulch** — pests often disturb or reduce mulch during infestation
- [ ] Plant **companion crops** if you hadn't already (marigold, basil)
- [ ] Set up **bird perches** (bamboo poles with horizontal stick on top) — birds eat remaining caterpillars
- [ ] Check if pest source was external (neighbor's unmanaged field?) — plant dense border hedges
- [ ] Review: Was the crop monoculture? If yes, plan intercropping next season.
- [ ] Log everything: which pest, which formulation worked, how many applications, timeline. This data is gold.

## Pest Quick-Reference Table (Bangladesh Common Pests)

| Pest | Crops Affected | First Sign | ZBNF Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Brown Plant Hopper (BPH)** | Rice | Yellowing at base, "hopper burn" | Neemastra spray at base + drain water temporarily |
| **Rice Stem Borer** | Rice | Dead heart (central shoot dies) | Agniastra + light trap (bulb over water basin at night) |
| **Aphids** | Vegetables, pulses | Curled leaves, sticky residue | Neemastra + encourage ladybugs |
| **Fruit Borer** | Tomato, brinjal | Holes in fruit, frass (droppings) | Agniastra + Brahmastra + hand-pick affected fruits |
| **Whitefly** | Vegetables, cotton | Tiny white insects fly when disturbed | Neemastra + yellow sticky traps (yellow plate + oil) |
| **Leaf Roller** | Rice, vegetables | Leaves rolled with silk thread | Agniastra + hand-pick rolled leaves |
| **Root Knot Nematode** | Vegetables | Stunted growth, root swellings | Marigold companion planting + Jeevamrutha drench |
| **Cutworm** | Seedlings | Seedlings cut at base overnight | Ash ring around seedlings + Agniastra soil drench |
| **Slugs/Snails** | Leafy vegetables | Holes + slime trails | Ash barrier around plot + hand-pick at dawn |
| **Mealybug** | Fruits, vegetables | White cottony masses | Neemastra + neem oil spray (neem seed kernel extract) |

## Physical/Mechanical Methods (Zero Cost)

| Method | How | For What Pests |
|---|---|---|
| **Hand-picking** | Inspect daily, pick visible insects/egg clusters | Caterpillars, beetles, egg masses |
| **Yellow sticky traps** | Yellow plate/card coated with mustard oil | Whiteflies, aphids, leaf miners |
| **Light traps** | Bulb over water basin at night | Moths, stem borers, hoppers |
| **Ash application** | Sprinkle wood ash on leaves and around base | Slugs, snails, some beetles |
| **Shaking** | Shake plants early morning (insects are sluggish) over a tray | Beetles, weevils |
| **Pheromone traps** | Available at DAE offices (often free for farmers) | Specific moth species |
