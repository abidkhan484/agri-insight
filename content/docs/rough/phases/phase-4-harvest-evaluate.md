# Phase 4: Harvest & Evaluate (Month 5–6)

> **Skill Level:** 🟢 Beginner to 🟡 Intermediate
> **Time:** 2–4 weeks
> **Goal:** Harvest, measure results, compare ZBNF vs chemical farming, document proof

---

## 🎯 Phase Objective

The moment of truth. Harvest your first ZBNF crop, weigh the yield, calculate costs, and compare everything with your chemical farming baseline. This data is your proof — for yourself, your family, and your community.

---

## Harvest Week Tasks

### Pre-Harvest (3–5 days before)

- [ ] 🟢 Stop all sprays (Neemastra, Agniastra) at least 7 days before harvest
- [ ] 🟢 Final Jeevamrutha was applied 7+ days ago
- [ ] 🟢 Prepare clean containers/bags for harvest
- [ ] 🟢 Arrange drying space (clean concrete floor or tarpaulin)

### Harvest Day

- [ ] 🟢 Harvest crop at the right maturity stage (don't harvest too early or too late)
- [ ] 🟢 **Weigh everything** — total harvest in kg from the ZBNF plot
- [ ] 🟢 If you have a chemical control plot — harvest and weigh that separately
- [ ] 🟡 Do a **taste test** — ZBNF produce often tastes noticeably better (more flavor, crunchier)
- [ ] 🟢 Take photos of the harvest with your plot visible in background
- [ ] 🟢 Note the harvest date

### Post-Harvest (1–2 weeks)

- [ ] 🟢 Proper drying (for grains) — clean, shaded drying area
- [ ] 🟢 Proper storage — dry, pest-proof containers
- [ ] 🟡 Sell or consume promptly for vegetables (no chemical preservatives = shorter shelf life)
- [ ] 🟢 **DO NOT burn crop residue** — leave it on the field as mulch or chop and spread

## Plan Next Season

- [ ] 🟢 Decide: will you expand ZBNF area? (Recommended: 2–3x the test plot)
- [ ] 🟢 If continuous cropping: apply Jeevamrutha to soil, add mulch, and plant the next crop immediately
- [ ] 🟡 If fallow period: sow a **cover crop** (dhaincha, cowpea, or mung bean) to keep soil biology alive and fix nitrogen
- [ ] 🟢 Never leave soil bare between seasons — always cover with mulch or living plants

---

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Analysis | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ZBNF yield much lower than chemical | Soil still transitioning; microbes need 2-3 seasons to rebuild | Continue ZBNF — yields improve each season. Increase Jeevamrutha frequency. |
| Produce doesn't sell well | Market doesn't recognize organic premium | Sell at direct-to-consumer markets, build organic buyer contacts, label as "chemical-free" |
| Soil health didn't improve | 1 season may not be enough (especially if soil was heavily chemical-dependent for years) | Apply extra Jeevamrutha, add vermicompost top-dressing, ensure mulch is always present |
