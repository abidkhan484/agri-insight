# Phase 3: Growing Season (Month 2–4)

> **Skill Level:** 🟡 Intermediate
> **Time:** 2–3 months (ongoing maintenance)
> **Goal:** Maintain ZBNF inputs, manage pests naturally, observe soil improvement

---

## 🎯 Phase Objective

Your crops are growing. Now you maintain the ZBNF system — regular Jeevamrutha, preventive pest sprays, mulch replenishment, and careful observation. This is where discipline matters.

---

## Weekly Task Schedule

### Every 15 Days — Jeevamrutha Application

- [ ] 🟢 Prepare a fresh batch of Jeevamrutha (same recipe as Phase 2)
- [ ] 🟢 Apply to soil through irrigation or direct pour at root zone
- [ ] 🟡 Alternate: every other application, do a 10% foliar spray instead of soil drench
- [ ] 🟢 Log the date and quantity in your farm diary

### Every 14 Days — Preventive Neemastra Spray

- [ ] 🟡 Prepare Neemastra: 200L water + 10L cow urine + 2 kg cow dung + 10 kg neem leaf paste
- [ ] 🟡 Mix → cover → stir twice daily → ferment 48 hrs → filter
- [ ] 🟢 Spray directly on crops (no dilution) — early morning or late evening
- [ ] 🟢 This is **preventive** — spray even if you see no pests

### Weekly — Mulch Check

- [ ] 🟢 Walk the plot and inspect mulch layer
- [ ] 🟢 Is it still 3+ inches thick? If decomposed or displaced → add more
- [ ] 🟢 Check for mold under mulch in monsoon — if excessive, reduce thickness to 2 inches and allow air

### Weekly — Observation Log

- [ ] 🟢 **Pest check**: Look under leaves, check stems, look at leaf tips. Note any pests seen (type + severity: none/low/medium/high)
- [ ] 🟢 **Soil moisture**: Finger test 2 inches below surface. Score: dry / moist / wet
- [ ] 🟢 **Earthworm activity**: See any castings on the surface? More over time = improving
- [ ] 🟢 **Plant health**: Leaf color, growth rate, any wilting/yellowing
- [ ] 🟢 **Weather notes**: Rainfall, temperature extremes

---

## Pest Response Protocol (ZBNF Only — No Market Inputs)

> See `docs/pest-management/emergency-pest-response.md` for the full guide.

| Severity | Action |
|---|---|
| 🟢 No pests / very few | Continue preventive Neemastra every 14 days |
| 🟡 Moderate (visible damage on 10-20% plants) | Apply Neemastra immediately + increase frequency to every 7 days |
| 🔴 Heavy (widespread damage >30% plants) | Apply **Agniastra** (chilli + garlic + tobacco + neem in cow urine, boiled). See pest-management docs for recipe. |
| 🔴🔴 Severe / critical | Apply **Brahmastra** (5-leaf formula). If still uncontrolled, apply Dashparni Ark (10-leaf extract). Consult `docs/pest-management/` |

## Monthly Deep Checks

### Month 2 — First Progress Assessment

- [ ] 🟡 Dig 1 sq ft, 6 inches deep — count earthworms. Compare with Phase 1 baseline.
- [ ] 🟢 Check water absorption time. Is it faster than baseline?
- [ ] 🟢 Smell the soil. Is the earthy smell stronger?
- [ ] 🟡 Take photos — compare with Phase 1 photos

### Month 3 — Mid-Season Check

- [ ] 🟡 Compare plant growth with control plot (chemical plot if you have one)
- [ ] 🟢 Evaluate input costs so far vs what you'd normally spend on chemicals
- [ ] 🟡 Check if living mulch (dhaincha/cowpea) needs trimming — cut and leave as mulch
- [ ] 🔴 If any crop is showing severe nutrient deficiency (very yellow, stunted), apply **Sour Buttermilk Spray** (1 part sour curd : 10 parts water) as emergency foliar feed

### Month 4 — Pre-Harvest Preparation

- [ ] 🟢 Stop Neemastra spray 7 days before harvest (residue clearance)
- [ ] 🟢 Continue Jeevamrutha until 1 week before harvest
- [ ] 🟢 Plan post-harvest: Will you immediately replant (continuous ZBNF) or leave fallow with cover crop?
- [ ] 🟡 Prepare mulch material in advance for next crop

## Season-Specific Adjustments (Bangladesh)

| Season | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| **Monsoon (Jun–Oct)** | Reduce Jeevamrutha to every 20 days (rain provides moisture). Watch for waterlogging. Reduce mulch to 2 inches to prevent mold. |
| **Winter/Rabi (Nov–Feb)** | Increase Jeevamrutha to every 10 days (cooler temp = slower microbial activity). Thick mulch (4 inches) to retain warmth. |
| **Hot/Pre-monsoon (Mar–May)** | Apply Jeevamrutha through irrigation (early morning only). Maximum mulch (4+ inches). No mid-day watering. |

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely Cause | ZBNF Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Yellow leaves | Nitrogen deficiency (soil microbes still building up) | Increase Jeevamrutha frequency to every 7 days. Sour buttermilk foliar spray. |
| Stunted growth | Soil transition — microbes haven't fully colonized | Be patient. Add extra mulch for organic matter. Consider vermicompost top-dressing. |
| Heavy pest attack | Ecosystem still unbalanced (no natural predators yet) | Escalate: Neemastra → Agniastra → Brahmastra. Plant marigold/basil as companion. See pest-management docs. |
| Mulch rotting/mold | Too thick in wet conditions | Reduce to 2 inches during monsoon. Allow air circulation. |
| Weeds growing through mulch | Mulch too thin | Add more mulch (4 inches minimum). Living mulch helps suppress weeds. |
